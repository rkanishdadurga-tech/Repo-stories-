## The Only One Who Stayed

- Arjun was known by almost everyone in school.

He was the type of boy who could make friends easily. He was funny, confident, and always knew the right thing to say.

At least, that was what people thought.

The truth was that Arjun never took relationships seriously.

There was a girl from school.

A girl from his neighborhood.

A girl from tuition.

A girl he met during his daily bus rides.

Each of them believed they were special to him.

Arjun never stopped to think about how much his words meant to them.

Among all of them, there was one girl named Nila.

Nila loved him sincerely.

When her friends warned her about Arjun, she refused to listen.

"He's not like that," she would say.

"You only see his mistakes."

Her friends weren't convinced.

But seeing how happy she was, they stopped arguing.

For Nila, Arjun wasn't just another boy.

He was her world.

One night, everything changed.

Arjun's phone rang.

It was almost midnight.

The screen showed Nila's name.

He answered lazily.

"What is it?"

For a few seconds, she didn't speak.

Then her voice came quietly.

"Is it true?"

"What?"

"The things people are saying about you."

Arjun sighed.

"Nila, just say it."

She took a deep breath.

"How many girls are there?"

Arjun stayed silent.

The silence answered her question.

Tears filled her eyes.

"I loved you honestly."

Still, he said nothing.

"Was any of it real?"

The questions irritated him.

He hated feeling cornered.

He hated being questioned.

And without thinking, he said words he would remember forever.

"If you like me, stay with me."

He paused.

"If not, leave."

His voice became colder.

"Do whatever you want."

Before she could answer, he ended the call.

Then he went to sleep.

The next morning, school felt strange.

Groups of students were whispering.

Some looked angry.

Some looked heartbroken.

Arjun frowned.

"What happened?"

Nobody answered.

Finally, one of his classmates spoke.

"We're going to Nila's house."

"Why?"

The boy looked at him in disbelief.

"You seriously don't know?"

Arjun felt his stomach tighten.

"Nila is gone."

The world around him froze.

The words refused to make sense.

Gone.

How?

Why?

His mind replayed the previous night's conversation again and again.

When he reached her house later, people filled every room.

Her friends were crying.

Her family looked shattered.

Nobody spoke to him.

Nobody wanted to.

As flowers were placed around her photograph, Arjun stood silently at the back.

Then he noticed something.

Nila.

She was standing beside the wall.

Looking directly at him.

Smiling.

Arjun's heart nearly stopped.

He blinked.

She was still there.

Nobody else seemed to notice.

Nobody else looked surprised.

Only him.

Fear rushed through his body.

When he looked again, she was gone.

That night he couldn't sleep.

He convinced himself it was grief.

Nothing more.

But the next day, he saw her again.

And the day after that.

And the day after that.

One week passed.

Nila became a part of his everyday life.

While he sat in class, she would sit beside him.

While he walked home, she would walk next to him.

While he ate lunch alone, she would sit across from him.

Sometimes she laughed.

Sometimes she cried.

Sometimes she simply watched him.

Nobody else could see her.

At school, things got worse.

Students stopped talking to him.

Friends avoided him.

Whenever he entered a room, conversations ended.

People had already decided who was responsible.

Nobody wanted his side of the story.

Nobody wanted explanations.

Nobody wanted to listen.

Arjun became alone.

Completely alone.

Except for Nila.

One afternoon, while sitting alone in class, Arjun accidentally smiled at something she did.

The entire classroom stared at him.

Some shook their heads.

Others whispered.

Arjun lowered his eyes.

He stopped caring.

The person he wanted to explain himself to was already gone.

Months passed.

Arjun changed.

He stopped talking to other girls.

He apologized to everyone he had hurt.

Some accepted.

Some didn't.

He understood.

He deserved that.

One evening, after a particularly difficult day, Arjun sat alone under a tree.

The weight of guilt, loneliness, and regret felt unbearable.

For the first time, he spoke openly.

"I'm tired."

Nila sat beside him.

"I'm tired of being hated."

She listened quietly.

"I'm tired of carrying this."

Tears filled his eyes.

"I don't know what to do anymore."

Nila gently reached for his hand.

For the first time, he felt warmth.

Real warmth.

And then she spoke.

"Live."

Arjun looked up.

"What?"

"You have to live."

Her voice was calm.

"You have to become better."

Tears rolled down his face.

"Why?"

Nila smiled sadly.

"Because that's what I wanted from the beginning."

Silence filled the air.

Then she added softly,

"I don't want your life to end."

Arjun cried harder.

The guilt remained.

The regret remained.

But for the first time, hope appeared too.

Years passed.

Nila never left.

She was there during his failures.

She was there during his successes.

She was there on lonely nights.

She was there when nobody else was.

Arjun never knew whether she was real, a memory, or simply a part of his heart refusing to let go.

Eventually, he stopped asking.

The answer didn't matter anymore.

Because everyone else had left.

Everyone else had judged him.

Everyone else had decided his story without listening.

But Nila stayed.

And little by little, she helped him become someone better than the boy he used to be.

Whenever he looked at her, she smiled.

And whenever he smiled back, he remembered the lesson that changed his life forever:

Words can hurt.

Words can heal.

And sometimes, the person who has every reason to leave is the one who stays.
